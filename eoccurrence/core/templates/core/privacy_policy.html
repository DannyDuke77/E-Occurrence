Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how e-Occurrence collects, uses, and protects the information you provide while using the platform. By accessing or using e-Occurrence, you agree to the terms of this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly (such as case details, user accounts, and contact information) as well as technical data (such as IP address, browser type, and usage logs) for security and audit purposes.

3. Use of Information

The information collected is used to:

  • Enable case recording and tracking
  • Facilitate collaboration between police, courts, and the public
  • Maintain security and prevent unauthorized access
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations

4. Data Protection

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data against unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure. However, no system can be completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute protection.

5. Data Sharing

Information entered into e-Occurrence may be shared with authorized police and court personnel as required for case management. Limited case status information may be accessible to the public, but sensitive personal data will remain protected.

6. User Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal information subject to legal and institutional requirements. Requests can be made through the relevant authorities managing e-Occurrence.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page, and continued use of the platform will signify your acceptance of the updated policy.

By using e-Occurrence, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to this Privacy Policy.
